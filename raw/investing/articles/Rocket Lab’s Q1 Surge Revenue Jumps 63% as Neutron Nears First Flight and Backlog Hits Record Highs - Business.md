Rocket Lab’s Q1 Surge: Revenue Jumps 63% as Neutron Nears First Flight and Backlog Hits Record Highs

[Rocket Lab](https://24newslatest.com/tag/rocket-lab "Rocket Lab") (NASDAQ:RKLB) delivered a first-quarter earnings report that surprised even the optimists. Revenue hit $200.3 million, up 63.5% from a year ago, and the company’s Q2 guidance of $232.5 million at the midpoint came in 12% above what analysts had penciled in. The non-GAAP loss of $0.02 per share was a penny better than expected, but the real headline was the sheer breadth of momentum across the business.

CEO Peter Beck pointed to a [record backlog](https://24newslatest.com/tag/record-backlog "Record Backlog"), a record cash position, and a record number of launch contracts signed across the Electron, HASTE, and upcoming Neutron programs. “We’re seeing broad-based demand from defense, commercial, and civil space customers,” Beck said during the earnings call. The quarter also saw the completion of several major contracts, the introduction of new in-house satellite technologies, and the integration of the recently acquired Motive Space Systems.

“This isn’t just a launch company anymore,” said Mark Chen, a space industry analyst at Orbital Insights. “They’re building a vertically integrated [space systems](https://24newslatest.com/tag/space-systems "Space Systems") powerhouse, and the numbers are starting to reflect that.” But not everyone is sold on the valuation. “Sure, the revenue is growing, but they’re still losing money,” said Linda Torres, a retail investor and former aerospace engineer. “I get the hype around Neutron, but at $99 a share, this thing is priced like it’s already won the space race. One failed test flight and the stock could get cut in half.”

CFO Adam Spice cautioned that capital expenditures would remain elevated as the company ramps up testing and production infrastructure ahead of Neutron’s first flight. “We’re investing heavily in scaling our satellite platform production and expanding into European markets,” Spice said. The company’s acquisition of Mynaric, a laser communications firm, is also expected to bolster its offerings in the satellite subsystems segment.

Looking ahead, analysts are closely watching three key milestones: the pace of Neutron’s development and test campaign, the conversion of the [record backlog](https://24newslatest.com/tag/record-backlog "Record Backlog") into recognized revenue—especially from satellite and hypersonic test contracts—and the successful integration of recent acquisitions like Motive and Mynaric. “If Rocket Lab can execute on Neutron and keep winning those big defense contracts, they’re going to be a serious competitor to SpaceX in the medium-lift market,” said Chen.

[Rocket Lab](https://24newslatest.com/tag/rocket-lab "Rocket Lab") shares closed at $99.53 on Thursday, up from $78.70 just before the earnings release. The stock has more than doubled over the past year, driven by growing confidence in the company’s ability to scale beyond small launches.
