# Rocket Lab (NASDAQ: RKLB)

US aerospace company. Operates Electron (small launch vehicle) and is developing Neutron (medium-lift). Expanding via acquisitions into satellite subsystems, hypersonic testing, and laser communications. CEO: Peter Beck. CFO: Adam Spice.

## Business Model

Rocket Lab describes itself as a "vertically integrated space systems" company — launch is one revenue line among many. It is systematically acquiring satellite component businesses to reduce dependence on launch alone.

## Launch Vehicles

| Vehicle | Class | Status |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Electron | Small lift (<300 kg) | Operational; multiple launches per year |
| HASTE | Hypersonic test vehicle | Suborbital; active defense contracts |
| Neutron | Medium lift (~13,000 kg) | Nearing first flight |

## Financials (Q1 2026)

- Revenue: $200.3M (+63.5% YoY)
- Q2 guidance: $232.5M midpoint (12% above analyst consensus)
- Non-GAAP EPS: -$0.02 (beat by $0.02)
- Record backlog, cash position, and launch contracts signed

## Key Acquisitions

- **Motive Space Systems** — spacecraft systems
- **Mynaric** — laser communications for satellite subsystems

## Investment Context (May 2026)

Stock at $99.53 post-Q1, up from $78.70 pre-earnings; doubled vs one year prior.

Bull case: Neutron execution + defense contract wins + backlog conversion + satellite subsystem revenue scaling.  
Bear case: Still unprofitable; priced at premium implying near-flawless execution on Neutron.
