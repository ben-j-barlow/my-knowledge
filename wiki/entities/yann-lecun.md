# Yann LeCun

Chief AI Scientist at Meta; founder of AMI Labs. Turing Award winner (2018, shared with Hinton and Bengio) for contributions to deep learning. One of the most prominent and consistent critics of the idea that LLMs are the path to human-level intelligence.

## Core Position

LLMs are commercially useful but architecturally incapable of general intelligence. The right path is world models — systems that learn abstract representations of how the world evolves and can predict the consequences of actions. He has held and published this view for over a decade, largely against the prevailing consensus.

## Key Contributions

- **Convolutional neural networks (CNNs)** — foundational architecture for computer vision
- **JEPA (Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture)** — his proposed architecture for learning world models via abstract latent space prediction rather than pixel-level generation
- **"Superhuman Adaptable Intelligence" (SAI)** — preferred framing over AGI; defines intelligence as the ability to solve problems you weren't trained on

## AMI Labs

Founded [AMI Labs](ami-labs.md) (Advanced Machine Intelligence, pronounced "ah-mee") through Zetta Ventures. Focus: JEPA-based world models for industrial and physical-world AI — factories, robotics, wearables, healthcare.

## Positions on Current AI Discourse

- Chain-of-thought is a workaround, not reasoning
- Scaling LLMs will not produce general intelligence
- Agentic systems without action-consequence prediction are fundamentally unreliable
- The physical economy — manufacturing, logistics, healthcare — is the real opportunity, and it requires world models
