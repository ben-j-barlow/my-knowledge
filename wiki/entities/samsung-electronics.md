# Samsung Electronics

South Korean conglomerate with dominant positions across DRAM, NAND, contract foundry (Samsung Foundry), and consumer electronics. Crossed $1T market cap on May 6, 2026 — the first trillion-dollar company in South Korea (the second in Asia, after TSMC).

## Semiconductor Division

### Memory
- **Global DRAM market share**: ~40%
- **HBM position**: Second to SK Hynix; ~30%+ of HBM market
- **HBM4**: First to announce mass production and commercial shipments (February 12, 2026); 1c (10nm-class) process; 4nm logic base die; 11.7 Gbps (46% above JEDEC 8 Gbps standard)
- **HBM4E**: Samples planned Q2 2026
- **2026 HBM outlook**: Sales expected to more than triple vs 2025; customer demand fulfillment at record lows; 2027 supply already being booked

### Foundry
- **SF3 node**: ~20–25k WPM at low yields, primarily for internal use (dramatically behind TSMC)
- CEO Jun Young-hyun (March 2026): Samsung is "the world's only semiconductor company capable of providing one-stop solutions from logic to memory, foundry, and packaging"

## Financials (Q1 2026)

- R&D: 11.34T won ($7.61B), +25.5% YoY
- Capex: 11.23T won ($7.54B); Device Solutions (semiconductors): 90.7% of total
- Full-year 2026 investment: >110T won ($73.8B) planned

## Labor Risk: Strike Threat

- **National Samsung Electronics Union**: 50,000+ workers; walkout scheduled May 21–June 7, 2026 (18 days)
- **Demands**: Scrap performance-bonus cap; redirect 15% of operating profit to workers (~45T won/year)
- **Precedent**: April 23 half-day rally cut memory output 18.4%, foundry output 58%
- **JPMorgan estimate**: Full 18-day walkout → annual operating profit down >40T won
- 1,700 suppliers have contingency plans; Suwon District Court injunction request pending

## Legal / Regulatory

- US VEU exemption removed September 2025; annual license required for China operations
- 86 patent suits in US in 2024 (+70% YoY); Netlist HBM-specific suit ongoing
