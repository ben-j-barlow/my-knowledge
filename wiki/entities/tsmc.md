# TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company)

The world's dominant contract chipmaker. TSMC manufactures chips designed by fabless companies — Apple, Nvidia, AMD, Qualcomm — and its process node leadership and advanced packaging capabilities make it effectively irreplaceable in the current AI semiconductor stack.

## Current Position (May 2026)

TSMC holds a commanding lead at leading-edge nodes (3nm, N2) and advanced packaging (CoWoS, SoIC). Its main competitors — Samsung Foundry and Intel Foundry — are operating at roughly 1/8 of TSMC's capacity at comparable nodes and at significantly lower yields.

## Process Node Roadmap

| Node | Capacity Target | Notes |
|------|----------------|-------|
| 3nm | 190k WPM by Q4 2026; 230k by 2027 | 25% CAGR 2022–2027 |
| N2 | 70% CAGR 2026–2028 | 5 fabs simultaneously; defect density target 2 quarters ahead of 3nm ramp schedule |

Comparison: Samsung SF3 / Intel 18A each at ~20–25k WPM, low yields, primarily internal use.

## Advanced Packaging

- **CoWoS** (Chip on Wafer on Substrate): yield >98%; 80% CAGR capacity growth through 2027 — the dominant method for packaging AI accelerators with HBM
- **SoIC** (System on Integrated Chips, 3D stacking): 90% CAGR capacity growth through 2027

Intel's competing EMIB-T: 80–85% pilot yield, must reach 95% for mass production by mid-2027.

## Competitive Moat

- Technology transfer times cut 20% at N2 vs 3nm ramp
- Each node builds on yield, tooling, and supply chain expertise of prior nodes
- Apple M-series migration risk is low given Samsung/Intel node capacity constraints
