# Source: Treasury Yields Are Testing The AI Equity Rally

**Author:** Agar Capital (Seeking Alpha)  
**Published:** 2026-05-17  
**Type:** Market commentary / portfolio strategy

## Summary

A portfolio manager argues that rising long-term Treasury yields are the primary threat to the AI equity rally, not deteriorating fundamentals. The 30-year Treasury has crossed 5% and the 10-year is at ~4.6%, compressing equity risk premiums to near zero (or negative) for high-multiple AI and semiconductor names. The Trump-Xi summit is characterised as a "cool-down" meeting that left all structural issues unresolved. Author reduces equity allocation from 100% to 75%, rotating out of high-beta semis and into defensive software, healthcare, and consumer staples.

## Key Claims

- **Bond market is reasserting control.** When rate rises coincide with inflation, deficits, new debt issuance, and political instability — rather than just strong growth — the valuation argument breaks down.
- **Equity risk premium is near zero for the S&P 500 and negative for the Nasdaq 100/Mag 7.** Little or no compensation for equity risk vs. holding Treasuries.
- **Equity duration explains semiconductor vulnerability.** Growth stocks with most of their value in future cash flows behave like long-duration bonds — rising discount rates compress their value regardless of earnings quality.
- **SOX at 25x projected earnings vs. 10-year average of ~19x.** Requires three simultaneous conditions to justify: high growth rates, flawless execution, and stable interest rates.
- **Trump-Xi summit was cosmetic.** Taiwan, rare earths, semiconductor export controls, military AI guardrails, and Hormuz/Iran all unresolved.
- **Software as defensive.** Capital-light, recurring-revenue software companies with pricing power have been outperforming semis during sell-off days — behaving more like value than growth.

## Notable Quotes

> "The reason I am reducing risk is not that company fundamentals have worsened. It is that the bond market has changed the price of money."

> "Enthusiasm can support multiples for some time, but it cannot fully offset a higher discount rate."

> "The more you pay for future profitability, the more susceptible your stock becomes to fluctuations in interest rates."
