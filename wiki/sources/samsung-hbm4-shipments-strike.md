# Samsung HBM4 Shipments Begin; 50,000-Worker Strike Threatens Supply Chain (May 2026)

> Source: https://www.techtimes.com/articles/316741/20260516/samsung-begins-hbm4-shipments-sk-hynix-lags-50000-worker-strike-five-days-threatens-ai-chip.htm
> Author: Allen Lee
> Published: 2026-05-17

## Summary

Samsung was first to mass-produce and ship HBM4 (February 2026), while SK Hynix is still ramping with only "timely ramp-up" language publicly. Simultaneously, a threatened 50,000+ worker strike at Samsung (May 21–June 7) is the single largest near-term risk to global AI chip supply. Both companies are accelerating R&D dramatically (Samsung +25.5% YoY, SK Hynix +68.3% YoY). The structural HBM capacity reallocation is creating the worst DRAM shortage in 15 years.

## Key Claims

### HBM4 Competitive Position
- **Samsung**: Mass production announced February 12, 2026; 1c (10nm-class) process; 4nm logic base die; 11.7 Gbps (46% above JEDEC 8 Gbps standard); HBM sales expected to more than triple in 2026
- **SK Hynix**: HBM4 "developing with key customers from early stages," preparing for "timely ramp-up"; no comparable public announcement; HBM4E samples H2 2026, mass production 2027; holds ~57% HBM market, ~70% of Nvidia's HBM orders (Counterpoint Research)

### Samsung Strike Threat
- **Union**: National Samsung Electronics Union; walkout scheduled May 21–June 7, 2026 (18 days); 50,000+ workers
- **Demands**: Scrap performance-bonus cap; 15% of operating profit to workers (~45T won/year)
- **Precedent**: Half-day rally April 23 cut daily memory output 18.4%, contract foundry output 58%
- **JPMorgan**: 18-day walkout could reduce Samsung annual operating profit by >40T won
- **1,700 Samsung suppliers** have contingency plans; Suwon District Court injunction request pending

### R&D and Capex (Q1 2026)
- **Samsung**: R&D 11.34T won ($7.61B), +25.5% YoY; capex 11.23T won ($7.54B); full-year 2026 investment >110T won ($73.8B)
- **SK Hynix**: R&D 2.55T won ($1.71B), +68.3% YoY; capex 7.35T won ($4.93B), +21.9% YoY; Cheongju packaging plant 19T won ($12.85B)

### DRAM Shortage Cascade
- HBM wafer displaces ~3 conventional DRAM wafers; Goldman Sachs 2026 supply-demand gap: 4.9% (worst in 15 years)
- Q1 2026 DRAM contract prices: +90–95% vs Q4 2025
- Gartner: DRAM prices +47% in 2026; DRAM+SSD combined +130% by end 2026
- Projected: ~17% rise in average PC prices; ~10.4% decline in PC unit shipments
- No meaningful consumer DRAM price relief before late 2027 or 2028 per Gartner, IDC, TrendForce

### Regulatory / Legal
- US Verified End User exemptions removed September 2025; annual licenses required for China operations
- Samsung: 86 patent suits in US in 2024 (+70% YoY); Netlist HBM suit ongoing
- SK Hynix: Monolithic 3D Section 337 action re: NAND/DRAM patent infringement
