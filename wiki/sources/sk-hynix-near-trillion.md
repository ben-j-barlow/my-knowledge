# SK Hynix: ~$50B From $1 Trillion (May 2026)

> Source: https://thenextweb.com/news/sk-hynix-cusp-trillion-dollar-hbm-rally
> Author: Allison Steffens Herrera (The Next Web)
> Published: 2026-05-14

## Summary

SK Hynix sits at ~$948B market cap after a 9x rally in under two years, driven entirely by its dominance in HBM. If it crosses $1T, South Korea becomes the first country outside the US to simultaneously host two trillion-dollar companies (Samsung crossed $1T on May 6). The article argues HBM is no longer a cyclical memory trade — it is structural exposure to the AI compute stack.

## Key Claims

- **Market cap**: ~$948B as of May 14; +200% YTD, +274% across 2025; up 9x in under 2 years
- **Samsung milestone**: Crossed $1T on May 6, 2026 — coincided with KOSPI breaking 7,000 for the first time
- **2026 HBM supply**: Confirmed sold out by CFO in October; likely shortage into 2027
- **Nvidia Vera Rubin**: SK Hynix expected to supply ~2/3 of HBM4 demand; mass production started February 2026; HBM4 expected to overtake HBM3E as revenue share from Q3 2026
- **Q1 2026**: Revenue nearly tripled YoY, crossed 50T won for the first time
- **Scale context**: Was below $100B market cap less than 2 years ago; now comparable to Walmart and Berkshire Hathaway
- **Structural argument**: Memory bandwidth — not raw compute — is the binding constraint for inference at scale; Big Tech's $725B 2026 capex must flow through HBM in the bill of materials
- **Risks**: Cyclicality, customer concentration, Samsung HBM4 yield catch-up as an ongoing quarterly risk

## Notable Quote

> "At $948bn, SK Hynix is no longer a memory company that benefits from AI. It is being valued as the structural counterparty to it."
